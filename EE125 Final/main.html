CASE STUDIES

Facebook-Cambridge Analytica Scandal

This scandal involved Facebook allowing the data of millions of users to be harvested by Cambridge Analytica without their consent, which was used for targeted political advertising

The key ethical concerns were about user consent, privacy violations, and the potential for using data to manipulate political opinions without transparency. The scandal exposed vulnerabilities in how personal data was handled by third-party companies.

Amazon's Rekognition

Amazon developed a facial recognition technology called Rekognition, which was marketed to law enforcement agencies. A study found that Rekognition misidentified people of color at a higher rate than white people, particularly in law enforcement applications.

The ethical questions here involve privacy rights, racial bias leading to possible misuse by authorities, and whether companies should provide technology to law enforcement without adequate safeguards.

Tesla's Autopilot

Tesla’s Autopilot feature has been linked to several accidents, some fatal, where the system failed to detect obstacles or the driver did not take control in time. Despite these incidents, Tesla continued to market Autopilot as a feature that could improve safety and reduce driver workload.

The ethical question revolves around whether Tesla was being transparent enough about the capabilities and limitations of its Autopilot system. Misleading marketing could encourage drivers to overestimate the technology’s autonomy and trust it in unsafe situations.

Google's Project Dragonfly

Google was working on a censored search engine for China, known as Project Dragonfly, which would comply with the Chinese government's strict censorship policies. This raised concerns about human rights and free speech, as Google would be enabling censorship of information that did not align with the Chinese government's views.

The ethical debate centered on whether Google's desire to expand into the Chinese market justified compromising on its values regarding privacy, freedom of information, and human rights.

Guides on Ethical Frameworks

  • IEEE Code of Ethics: A comprehensive guide for engineers focusing on public safety, integrity, and transparency.
  • ACM Code of Ethics: A framework for computing professionals emphasizing fairness, honesty, and respect for privacy.

Online Certification Programs

  • Ethics, Technology and Engineering: Self-paced course hosted by Coursera that discusses the broader ethical and social implications of engineering.
  • Impact, Ethics, and Issues with Generative AI: EdX course that explores the socioeconomic impacts of generative AI and related issues such as job and copyright implications.
